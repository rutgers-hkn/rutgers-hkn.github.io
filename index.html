About Us

We are proud to be the official international honors society for Electrical and Computer Engineers sponsored by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE). We help prepare students for the real world by hosting a variety of workshops and events. At Rutgers, we host a plethora of services for all Engineers. Information on them can be found below:

Tutoring

We host tutoring events and sessions upon request. With our network of members, we can always find someone to help.

Career Development

We host career building sessions, resume workshops and have real Engineers from industry come and share their experiences.

Skill Building

We host service programs and leadership training to educate students and make them more valuable to employers.

Research Track

Online, Spring 2023.

Professor Anand Sarwate, in a recent Zoom advising session, offered valuable insights on the path from undergraduate research to graduate school applications. During the virtual meeting, Professor Sarwate underscored the significance of undergraduates actively participating in research initiatives to cultivate essential skills and knowledge. He emphasized the importance of students initiating dialogues with professors and mentors to cultivate strong academic relationships, which can lead to impactful letters of recommendation. Furthermore, Professor Sarwate urged students to engage in introspection, encouraging them to contemplate their career aspirations and potential research interests, setting the stage for a successful trajectory toward graduate school.

Mini Bots

T. Schor Middle School, Spring 2017.

In 2017, a dedicated group of enthusiastic and talented undergraduate students from Rutgers University's HKN chapter generously volunteered their time and expertise to successfully coordinate and oversee the Mini-Bots competition hosted at T. Schor Middle School. This event marked a remarkable collaboration between higher education and local community engagement. The HKN undergraduates took on integral roles in the event's management, utilizing their knowledge and skills in engineering, robotics, and event organization to ensure its smooth execution. By contributing their time and energy, these students not only enriched the experience of the young participants but also demonstrated the spirit of mentorship and education, fostering a passion for technology and innovation among the next generation. The 2017 Mini-Bots competition stands as a testament to the positive impact that university students can have when they unite to promote learning, inspire young minds, and create meaningful connections between academic institutions and their communities.

Tutoring Night

Fall 2016

In a display of camaraderie and academic support, a group of dedicated undergraduates from Rutgers University's HKN chapter organized a dynamic tutoring night for their fellow peers. This initiative showcased the power of collaboration within the university community, as these driven HKN members utilized their extensive knowledge and expertise to provide invaluable assistance to their fellow undergraduates. By offering guidance across various subjects and fostering an environment of shared learning, these students not only facilitated a deeper understanding of course materials but also reinforced the sense of unity and mutual growth within the academic realm. The tutoring night exemplified the commitment of these HKN undergraduates to elevating the educational experience for all, illustrating how the spirit of mentorship and knowledge-sharing can create a supportive and thriving educational environment for everyone involved.
